About

I lead translational research projects at Stanford University's Holsinger Lab, where I've developed the first open oropharyngeal endoscopy segmentation dataset and built deep learning models for surgical data science. I've also worked as an ML Engineer building RAG systems and production ML pipelines for healthcare startup.

My research interests include multimodal representation learning, medical computer vision, vision-language models, and robust clinical AI. I'm particularly interested in reliable, data-centric approaches to modeling clinical data and deploying responsible ML systems for real-world healthcare applications.

I am currently completing my Master's in Data Science from Harvard Extension School and have a BS in Biology from UCLA.

Projects

NavigaTORS: Patient Education Chatbot

RAG System for Oropharyngeal Cancer
Developed a domain-specific RAG chatbot for HPV-associated oropharyngeal cancer patient education. Fine-tuned large language models with cancer-specific terminology and implemented safety guardrails. Pilot deployed with patient navigator materials.

Automated Multilabel ECG Classification

Deep Learning on MIMIC-IV-ECG
Built deep learning models (CNN, ResNet1D, Transformer hybrids) for multilabel ECG classification using 800k ECGs from 160k patients. Addressed severe class imbalance with ResNet1D achieving strongest AUC/AUPRC among tested architectures.

Tumor Segmentation from Head & Neck MRI

Semantic Segmentation Pipeline
Built semantic segmentation pipelines for gross tumor volumes in T2-weighted MRI. Implemented U-Net, Attention U-Net, U-Net++, and novel Mamba U-Net architectures. Preprocessed 3D volumes into 25k+ 2D slices with patient-level splits.
